# Stay Safe Online – Phishing Prevention Checklist

A practical guide to protecting yourself from phishing attacks — based on Task 5 research.

---

## 🛡️ Your Phishing Prevention Checklist

---

### ✅ 1. Always Verify the Sender's Email Address
Before acting on any email, check the full email address — not just the display name.

| What You See | What to Check |
|-------------|--------------|
| `SBI Customer Support` | Is the address `support@sbi.co.in` or `sbi-alert@secure-verify.net`? |
| `Google Account Team` | Is the domain `@accounts.google.com` or `@google-accounts.support`? |

> **Rule:** If the domain after `@` doesn't exactly match the company's official website, it's suspicious.

---

### ✅ 2. Hover Over Links Before Clicking
Never click a link in an email without first hovering over it to see the actual destination URL in your browser's status bar. 

- `sbi.co.in` → Legitimate  
- `sbi-secure-login.com` → Phishing

---

### ✅ 3. Ignore "Urgent Action Required" Pressure
Phishing emails deliberately create a sense of panic — "Your account will be closed in 24 hours!" A legitimate bank or service will **never** demand immediate action via email alone. Take a breath, open a new browser tab, and navigate to the official website directly.

---

### ✅ 4. Never Share OTPs, Passwords, or PINs — With Anyone
No bank, government agency, or legitimate company will ever ask for your OTP, ATM PIN, or full password via email, SMS, or phone call. Ever.

---

### ✅ 5. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on All Accounts
Even if a phisher captures your password, 2FA means they still cannot access your account without the second verification step. Enable it on your bank app, Gmail, GitHub, and LinkedIn — at a minimum.

---

### ✅ 6. Use a Password Manager and Unique Passwords
If you reuse the same password across multiple sites, one successful phish gives attackers access to all your accounts. A password manager (like Bitwarden or iCloud Keychain) generates and stores unique, strong passwords for each site.

---

### ✅ 7. Report Suspicious Emails — Don't Just Delete Them
- In Gmail: Click the three dots → "Report phishing"
- Forward suspicious emails to your bank's official phishing report address (e.g., `phishing@sbi.co.in`)
- Report cybercrimes at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) (India's national portal)

---

## 🚨 Quick Warning Signs of a Phishing Email

| Warning Sign | Why It Matters |
|-------------|----------------|
| Urgent or threatening subject line | Designed to make you panic and act without thinking |
| Generic greeting ("Dear Customer") | Real organisations know your name |
| Suspicious or mismatched sender address | The most reliable indicator of fraud |
| Links to unfamiliar domains | The actual destination may be a fake site |
| Requests for OTP, PIN, or password | No legitimate service ever asks for this |
| Poor grammar or spelling errors | Often a sign the email was mass-generated |
