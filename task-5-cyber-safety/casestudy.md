## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyberattack in which an attacker impersonates a trusted organisation — such as a bank, government agency, or popular online service — to deceive victims into voluntarily handing over sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or OTPs (One-Time Passwords). The name "phishing" is a play on the word "fishing," as attackers cast a wide net, hoping some victims will "take the bait."

---

## A Step-by-Step Hypothetical Scenario

**Victim:** Priya, a 20-year-old college student who uses online banking regularly.

1. **The Bait:** Priya receives an email that appears to be from her bank — "SBI Customer Support" — with the subject line: *"Urgent: Your account will be suspended within 24 hours."* The email logo, colours, and fonts look identical to SBI's official communications.

2. **The Hook:** The email instructs Priya to click a link to "verify her account immediately." The link reads `sbi-secure-verify.com` — not `sbi.co.in`. In her panic, she does not notice the difference.

3. **The Fake Page:** The link takes her to a website that looks exactly like SBI's login page. She enters her customer ID, password, and the OTP she receives on her phone, believing it is a legitimate verification process.

4. **The Theft:** Within seconds, the attacker captures her credentials and OTP, logs into her real bank account, and transfers ₹45,000 to a mule account before the session expires.

5. **The Discovery:** Priya receives an SMS transaction alert and realises what happened — but it is too late. The money has already been moved.

---

## Who is Usually Targeted?

Phishing attacks most commonly target:
- **Students and young adults** who transact online frequently but may not recognise subtle deception techniques.
- **Elderly users** who may not be familiar with URL verification or sender authentication.
- **Corporate employees** who receive high volumes of email and may click without scrutinising every message.
- **Anyone who has recently made a purchase or transaction**, as attackers leverage anxiety around pending orders or account issues.

---

## Consequences for the Victim

The consequences of a phishing attack can be severe and long-lasting:
- **Financial loss:** Immediate theft of funds from bank accounts or digital wallets.
- **Identity theft:** Stolen credentials may be used to open fraudulent loans or credit lines in the victim's name.
- **Privacy violation:** Email or social media accounts may be hijacked and used to attack the victim's contacts.
- **Psychological impact:** Victims often experience anxiety, shame, and a lasting distrust of digital systems.
- **Legal complications:** Resolving fraudulent transactions can take weeks or months and may require police reports and bank disputes.
